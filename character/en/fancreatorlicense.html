1. Purpose

These Terms are intended to allow individual creators who love TomaToma and respect its world to sell small-scale fan-made goods in order to supplement living expenses, tuition, creative activity costs, or similar needs.

These Terms do not waive any copyrights, trademark rights, or other rights relating to TomaToma and related content.

6. Scope of License

If an Eligible Creator complies with these Terms and the Fan Creation Guidelines, we grant that Eligible Creator a non-exclusive license to use the Character solely to the extent necessary to create and sell Eligible Goods.

This license is granted only to the Eligible Creator personally and may not be assigned, lent, transferred, or sublicensed to any third party.

This license does not grant the right to sell any item as official merchandise, authorized merchandise, affiliated merchandise, sponsored merchandise, or similar goods.

7. Annual Income and Annual Sales

In principle, an individual creator may sell Eligible Goods without prior approval and without paying a license fee if all of the following conditions are met.

  • The Eligible Creator’s personal Annual Income is equivalent to USD 30,000 or less.
  • Annual Sales from Eligible Goods are equivalent to USD 30,000 or less.
  • The Eligible Creator complies with these Terms and the Fan Creation Guidelines.

Annual Income and Annual Sales are based on the Eligible Creator’s self-reporting. We do not normally require supporting documents.

However, if we suspect false reporting, improper use, large-scale sales, disguised corporate use, or any other use contrary to the purpose of these Terms, we may request reasonable supporting materials.

8. License Fee for Sales Above the Threshold

If Annual Sales from Eligible Goods exceed the equivalent of USD 30,000, the Eligible Creator must pay us a license fee equal to 10% of the amount exceeding USD 30,000.

Exceeding the equivalent of USD 30,000 in Annual Sales does not immediately constitute a violation of these Terms. However, the Eligible Creator must self-report to us when the excess is recognized or within a reasonable period after the end of the applicable year.

Payment method, payment timing, exchange rate, and other necessary matters shall be as separately designated by us.
